# Official Tennis Polo (Toccer) Rules

## Objective
The objective of Tennis Polo (Toccer) is to score more goals than the opposing team by hitting the ball into the opponent's goal using a racket while adhering to the rules of the game.

## Equipment
- **Ball:** A lightweight ball that is easy to handle.
- **Rackets:** Each player uses a racket designed for striking the ball.
- **Goals:** Two goals are set at either end of the field.
- **Uniforms:** Teams must wear designated uniforms that are easily distinguishable.

## Teams
- Each team consists of 5 players on the field at any given time.
- Teams can have additional players on the bench for substitutions.

## Scoring
- A goal is scored when the ball completely crosses into the opponent's goal.
- Points may vary depending on the type of match or league rules.

## Field of Play Markings
- The field should be rectangular, with clearly marked boundaries, goal areas, and a center line.
- Key areas include the penalty area and a center circle for restart situations.

## Offside Rule
- A player is in an offside position if they are nearer to the opponent's goal line than the ball and the second-last opponent (usually the last defender) when the ball is played to them.

## Racket Keeper Rules
- The racket keeper is responsible for defending the goal and may not handle the ball outside the designated area.

## Match Duration
- Standard matches consist of two halves of 20 minutes each, with a 5-minute halftime.

## Touch Off
- The game begins with a touch off at the center of the field, with one team starting possession.

## Ball Out of Play
- The ball is out of play when it crosses the boundary lines or when a goal is scored.

## Tiebreak Mechanism
- In the event of a tie at the end of regular time, a sudden death tiebreak round of 5 minutes is played.

## Shot Clock Rule
- Teams have a maximum of 30 seconds to take a shot after gaining possession of the ball.

## 10-Second Rule
- Players must advance the ball past half-field within 10 seconds of gaining possession.

## Free Possessions on Interceptions
- Teams gain a free possession after successfully intercepting a pass made by the opposing team.

## Substitution
- Substitutions may be made at any stoppage of play, with players entering and exiting at the designated substitution area.

## Infractions Penalty Duration
- Minor infractions may result in a 1-minute penalty, while major infractions could result in a 2-minute penalty.

## Penalty Throws
- Penalty throws are awarded for certain fouls committed within the penalty area, taken from a designated spot.

## Unsportsmanlike Conduct
- Players exhibiting unsportsmanlike conduct may be penalized with a time penalty or ejection from the game.

## Historical Background
Tennis Polo, also known as Toccer, was created to combine elements of racquet sports with team play, drawing inspiration from traditional sports while allowing for innovative gameplay and strategies.
